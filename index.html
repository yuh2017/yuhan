About Me

I am Yu Han, a research associate at the Department of Land Economy at University of Cambridge. I hold a Ph.D. in Urban and Regional Planning from the University of Florida, along with master's degrees in Electrical and Computer Engineering from UF and Civil and Environmental Engineering from Carnegie Mellon University.

I am currently on the job market, seeking roles that align with my expertise in climate adaptation, transportation resilience, and natural hazard risk assessment. My research focuses on developing human-centered computational methods to evaluate community risks posed by natural disasters, such as coastal storm surges, hurricanes, and flooding.

I specializes in the development and application of human-centered computational approaches to evaluate systemic risks of natural disasters and climate change. My research focuses on quantifying compound risks and resilience dynamics across urban and coastal regions. Through the integration of agent-based modeling, geospatial analysis, and open data and tools, my research aims to inform climate resilience policies and enhance the capacity of societies to cope with and recover from climate-induced disasters.

My work has been published in prestigious journals such as Risk Analysis, Journal of Environmental Management, and Computers, Environment, and Urban Systems, among others.

In my spare time, I enjoy running, family time, and traveling.

Research Areas

My research develops data-driven techniques and micro-level urban simulation models to explore interactions between social and natural systems under compound climate hazards.

Disaster Resilience & Adaptation

Developing computational methods to assess community risks from natural disasters and evaluate adaptation strategies using agent-based models and geospatial analysis.

Resilient Transport Systems

Modeling travel behaviors and daily mobility under disaster scenarios to quantify cascading impacts and promote resilient transport systems.

Data-Driven Risk Assessment
