About Me

I develop human-centered computational methods to evaluate community risks from natural disasters and support equitable climate adaptation. My research integrates agent-based modeling, machine learning, and geospatial analysis to quantify compound risks and resilience dynamics in urban and coastal regions under climate change.

Research Theme 1: Integrating Social and Physical Systems to Measure Community Resilience.

I develop Agent-Based Models that couple natural hazard mapping with human behaviors and decision-making to assess the direct and indirect impacts of natural hazards. Using census data, human activity data, and natural hazard datasets, I build risk assessment tools for hydroclimate hazards such as flooding, hurricanes, and extreme heat, examining how these risks evolve under climate change, land use change, and population growth.

I also develop and apply AI- and data-driven approaches to explore the indirect impacts of natural hazards under the combined effects of climate change, land use transitions, and demographic dynamics, with the aim of identifying pathways to enhance community resilience.

Research Theme 2: Human-centric Tools for Urban Resilience Planning and Design

Integrating interdisciplinary knowledge into planning and design is challenging due to data compatibility issues, scientific uncertainty, and diverse planning goals. To address this, I develop techniques that fuse heterogeneous datasets and tools for scenario-based planning, decision support, and risk communication. Building on simulations at the intersection of natural and human systems and my experience with 3D visualization models, I aim to further integrate these components to inform adaptation planning and support public engagement through digital twin approaches.

I am developing digital twin models that merge simulation models, 2D/3D visualization, and multiple datasets to measure community risk, resilience, and adaptation strategies. These digital twins will integrate risk assessment tools from my previous natural hazard and resilience studies to enhance systemic risk assessment and risk communication.
