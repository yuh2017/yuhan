Disaster Resilience & Adaptation

Climate related hazards, such as floods, coastal storm surges, and heatwaves, increasingly threaten the safety and reliability of urban systems. Across the globe, we have witnessed how extreme weather can damage infrastructure, disrupt transportation systems, and affect local economies. These challenges are often compounded, where a city might face flooding followed by a heatwave, or a hurricane coupled with power outages, creating complex scenarios for policymakers. The lack of understanding such impacts to our cities creates significant obstacles for governments at all levels to develop effective adaptation plans and build resilient communities. On the other hand, while the direct impacts of hazards such as floods, hurricanes, and landslides are well-documented, the indirect consequences—resulting from the interdependencies between natural and social systems are often overlooked or underestimated. These cascading risks can amplify disaster impacts, spreading across systems due to changes in hazard patterns, human exposure, economic shifts, and disruptions in supply chains. My research seeks to address these gaps by examining how such cascading effects propagate across social, environmental, and infrastructure systems.

Key research questions to be explored in my research include:

  1. How do human risk mitigation behaviors, infrastructure investments, or land-use policies influence community resilience from natural hazards?
  2. How do adaptation policies alter a community’s resilience to natural hazards over years?

To quantify these systemic risks, I leverage advanced agent-based models, computational methods, and open geospatial tools to analyze the impacts of compound hazards—such as storm surges and flooding—on vulnerable communities. By integrating open data and geoinformation techniques, my work contributes to the development of a robust framework for assessing and managing the interconnected risks posed by climate-driven extreme events.

In addition, my research explores the role of adaptation planning in reducing the vulnerability of urban and rural communities to climate change. With rapid urbanization and changing climate, the urgency for effective adaptation strategies has never been higher. Through interdisciplinary approaches and scenario analyses, I assess the effectiveness of various mitigation and adaptation policies. Relying on machine learning and artificial intelligence (AI) techniques, I simulate the impact of climate change and natural hazards on communities, aiming to provide actionable insights for policymakers. The ultimate goal is to identify and design strategies that enhance resilience, inform sustainable urban planning, and guide proactive adaptation in the face of climate uncertainty.
