Data-Driven Risk Assessment

Relying on the asset-level data, household surveys, human activity data, and natural hazard data, I am applying data-driven techniques to explore future trends of urban development under the impacts of climate change and integrate them into my Agent-Based Modeling framework. I aim to explore innovative methodologies that leverage artificial intelligence (AI) to synthesize population data and simulate population activities. For example, I am using large language models (LLM) and machine learning-based approaches to generate individual activity patterns and assess behavioral during and after disasters, enabling a more comprehensive assessment of dynamic human exposure to compound and cascading events. The insights gained from this theme, particularly the understanding of individual and household responses to hazards, will directly inform the development of more realistic and human-centric adaptation strategies. The Findings will be visualized and communicated through the digital twin based approaches.

Meanwhile, I leverage cutting-edge methods from computer science, spatial statistics, and urban planning to address critical urban environmental issues and promote social resilience. This research involves extracting actionable insights from large-scale urban data using advanced data science techniques. By developing new geospatial models and analytical tools, my work focuses on improving the collection, processing, visualization, and analysis of urban data across diverse contexts—both in urban centers and rural areas. These tools are designed to support urban planning and management, with an emphasis on solving key social challenges such as inequality, vulnerability, and access to resources. Ultimately, my research aims to foster more resilient cities by providing innovative approaches to urban planning that prioritize sustainability, inclusivity, and adaptability.
