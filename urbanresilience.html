Resilient Transport Systems

Advancing Resilient and Adaptive Transport Systems in Urban Planning

Urban resilience is a critical focus in addressing the multidimensional challenges posed by climate change, rapid urbanization, and environmental uncertainties. My research contributes to this by developing data-driven approaches to enhance the resilience and adaptability of urban transport systems under climate stress. Employing an interdisciplinary lens, I integrate urban analytics and environmental planning principles into an Agent-Based Modeling (ABM) framework that simulates human behavior during climate-induced disruptions and adaptation benefits on urban transport system for a changing climate.

Key Research Questions

  • How do individual travel behaviors change in response to hydroclimatic disruptions such as flooding or extreme heat?
  • How can agent-based modeling be used to simulate and understand these behavioral dynamics at scale?
  • What adaptive strategies and design interventions can urban planners employ to mitigate climate impacts on mobility networks?

Using the open-source agent-based simulation framework MATSim, I develop large-scale simulations to assess transport disruption and resilience under various climate scenarios. By integrating OpenStreetMap, satellite imagery, and socioeconomic datasets, my research evaluates commuter behavior both before and after natural disasters, capturing the spatial-temporal dynamics of mobility under environmental stress.

This research adopts scenario planning techniques to explore interventions such as infrastructure retrofitting, adaptive transport management, and policy innovations for pre-disaster evacuation and post-disaster recovery. For example, NASA’s Black Marble VIIRS nightlight imagery is used alongside demographic and behavioral data to estimate evacuation patterns and recovery rates following extreme events.

Model outputs are evaluated using indicators such as commute delays, areas of the city affected by flooding, and shifts in public transit ridership. These evaluations support climate-resilient transportation planning and assist urban planners in designing systems that are robust to future climate uncertainties.

To complement the mobility analysis, I aim to incorporate economic impact assessment tools to measure the costs associated with transport disruption. This includes estimating the value of lost time in congestion, commuting delays, and infrastructure recovery costs.
